Overview

The BRCiS Real-time Risk Monitoring (RTRM)and Early Action System aims to provide timely and relevant community-driven early warning information to Consortium Members and anticipatory/early action stakeholders in Somalia. The system monitors climate and non-climate shocks and their effects to inform the triggering of early actions and Crisis Modifier activation. BRCiS collects monthly early warning data, particularly on disaster risks and community vulnerabilities, from 172 communities in 19 districts in Somalia with financial support from the Foreign, Commonwealth & Development Office and Swedish International Development Agency. To this end, the RTRM dashboard serves as a platform for accessing data on a broad range of key early warning indicators to facilitate monitoring, prioritization, consensus building, rapid adjustments, coherent and informed decision making. It continues to be improved and refined based on the very helpful feedback from our users.

Indicators, thresholds and red flagging

The system monitors of a set of qualitative and quantitative indicators to warn of shocks (i.e., drought, flood, desert locusts, acute watery Diarrhoea/cholera outbreaks and conflict) and another set to capture the impacts of these shocks (i.e., livelihood assets, market dynamics, migration and household coping strategies).Each indicator has three indicator thresholds to measure the level of emergency when experiencing a shock: “normal”, “alert” and “alarm”. Each indicator falls under one of these thresholds, changing as the severity of the event grows or weakens. BRCiS uses the indicators and thresholds to adopt a harmonized triggering approach of early action called “red flagging”. Combined Indicator and threshold weights are used to calculate the risk score of each of the target areas. An area receives a red flag if the calculated risk score is equal to or more than 50%.

 
 
 
 
 
Methodology
  • 1 - Data collection by Early Warning Early Action Committees (EWEA) with support from BRCiS EWEA focal points
  • 2 - Data is submitted on ONA (ODK platform)
  • 3 - Dashboard retrieves and analyses the data
  • 4 - Red flagged districts are identified
  • 5 - The EWEA information and shock trends depicted on the dashboard are communicated back to the EWEA committees for use in decision- making and triggering early actions
  • 6 - Members, EWEA committees and Community Resilience Committees (CRCs) with Red Flagged areas review, interpret and triangulate the data with secondary sources
  • 7 - Early actions activated for red flagged areas where relevant and subject to availability of funds
  • 8 - Early Actions Implemented and monitored

Risk Levels Actions to be Taken
Normal Risk score between 0-24%

- Continue standard risk monitoring protocols.

- Maintain active communication channels with community and stakeholders regarding current conditions.
Alert Risk score between 25-49%

- Intensify monitoring activities with increased frequency and depth.

- Notify the community of potential imminent risks and initiate preparatory actions.
Alarm Risk score between 50-100%

- Implement immediate red flagging protocols.

- Communicate the presence of immediate risk and activate response strategies.
